The Do's and Don't's of DHLab for critical essays:



DO find patterns and explore them!

Critical writing is all about looking at patterns and making conclusions based on those patterns. The beauty of DHLab is that it makes the search for these patterns much easier. Take advantage of that!



DON’T rely too heavily on text statistics in your essays.

Numbers aren’t going to do the heavy lifting of your analysis––you are! For example, if you assert that an author uses the dichotomy between light and dark to compare two characters, all our algorithms can do is show the prevalence of these themes and help you find passages about them. It’s up to you to actually analyze those passages on a deeper level.



DO make sure your input file (.txt) is clean

Sometimes there are recurring pieces of text in certain .txt files, like the title of the novel or page numbers. You don’t want those. These can mess up some of your statistics.



DON’T put statistics about your essay in your essay

The statistics you get from Thesis Analysis should be for your personal writing purposes. Don’t try flexing the quantitative complexity of your work!
