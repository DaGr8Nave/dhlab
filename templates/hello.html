Welcome to Pingry's Digital Humanities Text Analysis Lab , for use by English classes, Humanities Research Projects, and curious souls!

This website is meant to be a playground for Pingry's readers to look at creative and critical writing in an objective, numerical manner.

Why mix words and numbers?

The beauty of natural language processing and digital humanities is often hidden from the general population of writers and readers. While, of course, a computer could never read and analyze text better than a human, it has the capacity to make interesting connections and find useful patterns much faster than a human.
We designed these algorithms in order to bridge the gap, to educate and empower all readers and writers in a world where large-scale, automated data analysis is ever-more important.


Natural Language Processing (NLP):

the science of looking at large amounts of natural language with computers. It powers search engines, online translators, voice assistants, and much, much more. In the context of this website, NLP is a tool for the text analysis portion of digital humanities.

Digital Humanities (DH):

the application of coding and statistical tools in the context of humanities fields, most notably literature, philosophy, and history.
