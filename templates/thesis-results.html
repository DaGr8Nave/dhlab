Readability Metrics

The following information reports on the different parts of your essay in terms of readability. Make no mistake, the idea of readability and the complexity of writing is highly subjective; nevertheless, there have been indices established in order to give an objective idea of how 'sophisticated' a certain piece of writing might be, based on the lengths of words and sentences. Here are a few of them.

Flesch Reading Ease

206.83 - 1.015(avg_sentence_length) - 84.6(avg_syllable_count)

Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

0.39(avg_sentence_length) + 11.8(avg_syllable_count) - 15.59

Gunning-Fog Index

0.4(avg_sentence_length + percent_hard_words)
Hard words are defined as words that are greater than three syllables and are neither proper nouns, the combination of simpler words, nor 2 syllable words that are extended syllabically by -es or -ed.
